\frame{
Utility of strategy $\sigma\in\Delta S$ in population $\chi$:

$$
u(\sigma, \chi) = \sum_{s\in S}\sigma(s)u(s,\chi)
$$

\framebox{\textbf{Interpretation:} number of descendants of $\sigma$.}

\frame{
Consider a population where all individuals initially play $\sigma^*$. If we assume that a small proportion $\epsilon$ start playing $\sigma$. The new population is called the post entry population and will be denoted by $\chi_\epsilon$.
\begin{center}
\textbf{Evolutionary Stable Strategy:}
\end{center}
A strategy $\sigma^*\in\Delta S$ is called an \textbf{Evolutionary Stable Strategy} if $\exists$ $0<\bar\epsilon<1$ such that for every $0<\epsilon<\bar \epsilon$ and every $\sigma\ne\sigma^*$:
$$u(\sigma^*,\chi)>u(\sigma,\chi)$$
}
